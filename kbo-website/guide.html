Beginner Guide

KBO Beginner Guide

If you are new to Korean baseball, this guide explains the most important things to know before following the KBO.

1. What is KBO?

KBO stands for Korea Baseball Organization. It is the top professional baseball league in South Korea. It is similar to MLB in the United States, but it has its own teams, fan culture, and playoff system.

2. 10 Teams

The KBO has 10 teams based in different cities and regions of Korea. Each team has its own history, colors, stadium, and fan identity.

For new fans, choosing a team can be part of the fun. Some people choose by city, players, team colors, or the team’s overall style.

3. Season Format

The KBO regular season usually starts in late March. Each team plays 144 games, and the regular season is usually scheduled to end around mid to late September.

If games are canceled because of rain or other weather issues, those games are played later as remaining games. Because of this, the regular season can sometimes continue into early October.

After the regular season is fully finished, the postseason begins. The KBO uses a step-by-step playoff system where higher-ranked teams enter later rounds.

The postseason starts with the Wild Card round between the 4th and 5th place teams. The 4th place team has a big advantage because it starts with one win. The 5th place team must win both games to advance.

After that, the winner plays the 3rd place team in the Semi-Playoff. Then the winner of that round plays the 2nd place team in the Playoff round.

Finally, the 1st place team joins in the Korean Series. The Korean Series is the championship round, and the winner becomes the KBO champion.

4. Fan Culture

KBO games are known for their loud and energetic fan culture. Fans often sing team songs, chant for players, and cheer together during the whole game.

This makes KBO games feel very active and social. Even if someone does not know every rule of baseball, the cheering culture can make the game easier and more fun to enjoy.

5. Key Differences from MLB

KBO and MLB are both professional baseball leagues, but the experience can feel different. KBO games often have more organized cheering, a strong team-based fan culture, and a different postseason structure.

For international fans, understanding these differences can make it easier to enjoy KBO without expecting it to feel exactly like American baseball.
